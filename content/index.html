At the openSUSE Summit 2013 (oSSum13) a wide variety of Free and Open Source Software contributors will gather to collaborate on one of the major Linux distribution projects.

Program

The oSSum13 program includes keynotes, talks, workshops and a lot of fun. oSSum13 takes place November 15-17, 2013 following SUSEcon , our main sponsors' yearly conference. Registrations finalized before November 1st include free admission to Technology Showcase and Keynotes of SUSECon on Friday November 15.

Social Events

Geekos are a hard-working, fun-loving bunch. But even Geekos need a break to socialize and get some food in their bellies. That's why we bring you the Geeko Lounge. A place to relax, put your feet up and hang out with fellow Geekos. Each morning we serve breakfast, and throughout the day, we'll have refreshments to quench your thirst and nibble on. Couches and bean bags to plop your dupa on as you take a breather from the hustle and bustle of the busy day checking out sessions. We are also planning to add a party or two, so check back to get the latest info.

Location

The openSUSE Summit will be held November 15-17, 2013 in beautiful sunny (we hope!) Florida. Disney's Coronado Springs Resort will welcome all Geekos to this year's event. At the Disney Resort you are located right next door to one of the greatest attractions in Orlando. But Orlando has more to offer than just Disney World, it is also home to Sea World, Universal Studios and the Kenedy Space Center is only about 60 miles away. If you are interested in the various attractions you might want to check out an Orlando city pass.

Registration

Registration for the openSUSE Summit is now open. For the first time we will use the Open Source Event Manager (OSEM) to handle registration and session submission. We share the OSEM instance with the openSUSE Conference, thus please pay attention that you register for the event you intend to participate in. If you did not previously register for an event in OSEM you first need to create and account (this is a very simple process) and than register for the summit. So head on over to the Registration page and register for the openSUSE Summit.

Call for Participation

The quality of the event will largely be determined by the number and quality of sessions. Therefore, if you have a great idea please submit your session proposal here. We are looking for sessions covering openSUSE technology, the openSUSE community, as well as sessions covering FOSS projects that may not immediately be related to openSUSE.

Code of Conduct

We want to ensure that everyone attending the openSUSE Summit has a great experience in an enjoyable environment. Therefore, all particpants are expected to follow the Code of Conduct.
